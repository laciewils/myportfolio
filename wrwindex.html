What is William Rose Wines?

Overview.

William Rose Wines is a winery located in the Whiteaker neighborhood of Eugene, Oregon. The winery works closely with small vineyards to source the best grapes Oregon has to offer, providing them with a wide array of exciting varietals. Our goal is to create approachable, food-friendly wines crafted with a blend of Old and New world styles which reflect our winemaker’s globetrotting past. The website serves as a place for a current or potential consumer to find any relevant information about the brand, from the brand story to tasting notes. The site also serves as the place for industry professionals to find trade information. The website reflects the brand's unique personality that differentiates it from the local competitors.

Problem.

The challenge is to emphasize the brand identity without distracting from the essential information that the user is looking for. I needed to make sure that any information that users look for when visiting a winery website was available on the William Rose Wines site. In addition, I needed to discover what appeared to be pros and cons of similar sites. To do this, I researched the competitors and surveyed potential users.

Goal.

William Rose Wines site will allow users to find essential wine and company information while displaying a unique interface that portrays the brand identity that makes the company stand out.

User and Competitor Research

Research Process.

In the beginning stages of William Rose Wines research, I looked into potential competitors and questioned potential users. I noted the features the competitors did and did not have, as well as the feel and design of their interfaces. I then surveyed multiple users to get a feel of what applications they use for content storing, and what they like or are lacking from applications on the market. Together, this research provided me with a solid foundation to build upon. From the user research, three users exemplified the overall responses and thoughts shared by my survey respondents. These users also validated some of my own findings from the competitor research.

Competitive Analysis.

Sweet Cheeks

STRENGTHS: Landing page is simple, yet contains a lot of relevant information. Navigation is obvious and takes you to a relevant page. All buttons have same color for obvious calls to action. Informative footer. Allows you to connect with them in many ways.
WEAKNESSES: “See all varietals” button is redundant, takes you to page with same options rather than a page with photos of all wines. Map malfunction on contact page.
OPPORTUNITIES: Allow a way to easily view all wines, and narrow it down if wanted. Give user more straight forward way to help them pick a wine. Make all contact info easily visible upon one click in navigation.
THREATS: Larger company. Has a vineyard that can be visited. Has vineyard for large bookings such as weddings.

Capitello

STRENGTHS: One short, clear summary of their brand. Simple navigation bar that is clearly visible right away. Responsive content. Calls to action on home page send users to same pages as they do in nav bar. Address and phone number are quickly apparent.
WEAKNESSES: Some irrelevant content – there is an entire page for “shop” but there is a small blurb at the bottom of the page stating that purchasing of wines online isn’t currently available. The calls to action for shopping will lead the user to an action that can’t occur. Their “book our venue” button leads them to the top of the Events page, and they will have to scroll quite a bit down the page to actually find the booking section. Potentially overwhelming content in “Our Story” without important information bolded. No information about the wines besides name and price. Limited contact information and presented oddly above nav bar. No footer.
OPPORTUNITIES: Capitalize on showcasing what makes us unique and making it obvious. Give consumers detailed descriptions of the wines. Help consumers decide what wine they might like best. Make landing page more visually stimulating, less line work. Make site more personable.
THREATS: Customers have a go-to wine brand or wine bar and do not visit the site to begin with. Market saturation of wine brands. Rebranding could confuse existing customers.

J. Scott

STRENGTHS: They seem approachable and have photos that make the site seem more personable. Their content is relevant, and there is a good amount of content.
WEAKNESSES: Navigation bar placement is off. Logo in navigation isn’t reflective of wine branding. Structure of text and photos below hero lacks design appeal. Calls to action are just bolded text and not very obvious.
OPPORTUNITIES: Have a more clear, simple navigation structure. More professional looking pages, especially landing page. Have branding be consistent between product and website. Again, more descriptions of wines and help consumers select wines.
THREATS: Many wine brands. Have multiple locations. Ability to have events in barrel room, more of a winery feel and they showcase this.

Branding

Logo.

The William Rose Wines logo and brand concept comes from the inspiration of the steampunk genre - vintage industrial elements with a modern twist. The incorporation of the rose element is meant to bring a softened, elegant edge to this concept and to prevent it from becoming kitschy.

Color Palette.

The color palette for the site takes from the colors that you would naturally find at any winery in the tasting or barrel rooms. Natural colors from the barrels and wood, dark red from the red wines grapes, cream hues from the white wine grapes.

Typography.

For the fonts, I chose one serif and one sans serif. For my sans serif font, I wanted a font that is clean but not too geometric or modern. My serif font brings a vintage feel without being slanted or cursive-like.
